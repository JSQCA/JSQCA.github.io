Keeping Journal Square safe is a core part of the JSQCA's mission. Below you'll find emergency contacts, crime reporting resources, and information on pedestrian, bike, and community safety programs.

Report a Crime

For crimes in progress, always call 911. To report a crime after the fact or provide an anonymous tip, use the JCPD Tip Line at 855-JCP-TIPS or email policetips@njjcps.org.

For non-emergency quality-of-life issues like broken streetlights, graffiti, or illegal dumping, use SeeClickFix or call the Resident Response Center at (201) 547-4900.

Police Districts

Journal Square sits near the boundary of two JCPD districts. Use the Police Districts Map to confirm which district covers your address.

  • West District – 1 Jackson Street, Jersey City, NJ 07304
  • North District – 282 Central Avenue, Jersey City, NJ 07307

JCPD Headquarters is located at Two Jackson Sq. (356 MLK Drive).

Community Policing Programs

The JCPD Community Relations Unit runs several programs to build trust between officers and residents:

  • Coffee with a Cop – Informal meet-ups to break down barriers between police and citizens.
  • ARRIVE Together – Plain-clothed officers and mental health professionals jointly respond to mental health emergencies.

Pedestrian Safety & Vision Zero

Jersey City adopted Vision Zero, committing to eliminate all traffic fatalities and serious injuries on city streets. Improvements include daylighting intersections, narrowing lane widths, adding speed humps, and building protected bike lanes.

Safe Streets JC is one of the city's most active advocacy organizations working to achieve zero traffic deaths through education and community outreach.

Bike Safety

  • Protected Bike Lanes – Jersey City is expanding its protected bike lane network, including on Bergen Avenue between Journal Square and McGinley Square.
  • Bike JC – A volunteer-based nonprofit advocating for safer cycling, hosting group rides, and organizing the annual Jersey City Ward Tour.
  • Citi Bike – Bike-share stations throughout Journal Square. Find stations on the Citi Bike map.

Emergency Preparedness

  • Office of Emergency Management – Sign up for AlertJC to receive emergency notifications.
  • Community Emergency Response Team (CERT) – Volunteer to help prepare for and respond to emergencies like flooding, snowstorms, and power outages.

Get Involved

If you have safety concerns in Journal Square, attend one of our monthly meetings. We can connect you with the right city departments and advocacy organizations.

Meetings are held on the second Monday of every month at 7 PM at 71 Sip Ave., 6th Floor, Jersey City.
