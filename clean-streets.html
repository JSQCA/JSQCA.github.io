A clean Journal Square starts with each of us. Below you'll find resources for reporting issues, trash and recycling guidelines, and ways to get involved in keeping our neighborhood beautiful.

Report an Issue

See something that needs attention? Here's how to report it:

  • SeeClickFix – Jersey City's primary tool for reporting potholes, graffiti, illegal dumping, broken streetlights, overflowing trash, and other quality-of-life issues. Available online and on mobile.
  • Resident Response Center: (201) 547-4900 – Jersey City's one-stop office for city services, Monday–Friday 9 AM–5 PM.
  • Office of Code Compliance – For property maintenance violations including unkempt lots, accumulated trash, and building code issues.

Trash & Recycling

Look up your collection schedule on the Garbage Collection Map or visit the DPW Sanitation page.

  • Trash is collected twice per week. Place trash curbside between 7:00 PM and 10:00 PM the night before your scheduled collection day.
  • Recycling is collected once per week. Do not place recyclables in plastic bags.
  • Bulk items (up to 4 per collection day) – Call (201) 547-4400 to schedule. Mattresses must be wrapped in plastic.
  • Missed pickups – Report via SeeClickFix or call (201) 547-4400.

Street Cleaning

Jersey City runs a regular street sweeping schedule. Move your vehicle according to posted alternate side parking signs so sweepers can clean your block.

  • Street Cleaning Schedule – Look up your route on Jersey City Open Data.
  • Main Road Sweeper Routes
  • Follow @JCParking for real-time updates on parking suspensions.

Beautification Programs

  • The Great Jersey City Cleanup – An annual citywide cleanup held around Earth Day. Since 2016, nearly 6,000 volunteers have participated. Sign up at Serve Jersey City.
  • Jersey City Mural Arts Program (JCMAP) – Links artists with property owners to paint murals, reducing graffiti and beautifying the city. Over 200 large-scale murals citywide.
  • City of Trees – Join the "Tree Lovers Crew" (TLC) through training workshops to help care for Jersey City's urban canopy.
  • Sustainable Jersey City – Partners with JSQCA on monthly Community Green Committee meetings focused on sustainability and neighborhood cleanliness.

How You Can Help

  • Report problems promptly – The more residents report, the faster the city responds. Use SeeClickFix or call (201) 547-4900.
  • Follow proper trash practices – Put trash out between 7–10 PM the night before collection. Break down cardboard boxes. Don't bag recyclables.
  • Move your car for street sweeping – Check posted signs on your block.
  • Care for your block – Sweep sidewalks and gutters, maintain tree pits, and report code violations on neglected properties.
  • Volunteer – Sign up at Serve Jersey City to join cleanups, tree plantings, and park maintenance events.

Key Organizations

  • Sustainable Jersey City – Environmental sustainability, tree planting, cleanups, and composting programs.
  • Jersey City Parks Coalition – Park development, maintenance, and volunteer events.
  • Courthouse Park Advocacy Group – Advocating for green space development in the Journal Square area.
  • Hudson County Improvement Authority – County-level recycling programs and resources.

Get Involved

Have concerns about cleanliness in Journal Square? Attend one of our monthly meetings to raise issues and connect with fellow residents working to keep our neighborhood clean.

Meetings are held on the second Monday of every month at 7 PM at 71 Sip Ave., 6th Floor, Jersey City.
